# ðŸ“˜ Part 1: GCSE Research Methods Course
**Target Audience:** GCSE Students  
**Total Duration:** 8 Lessons (1 Hour Each)

## Lesson 1: The Basics of Investigation
**Topics:** Hypotheses & Variables
1.  **Hypotheses:** Definition, Null vs Alternative Hypotheses.
2.  **Variables:** Independent (IV), Dependent (DV).
3.  **Extraneous Variables:** Definition ("nuisance variables"), examples (intelligence, prior knowledge).
4.  **Practice:** Identifying IV/DV in scenarios (e.g., Music & Learning).

## Lesson 2: Selecting Participants
**Topics:** Sampling Methods
1.  **Target Population:** Definition.
2.  **Methods:**
    *   **Random:** Equal chance, drawing from hat/computer.
    *   **Opportunity:** Available people, fast but biased.
    *   **Systematic:** Every 'nth' person, unbiased selection.
    *   **Stratified:** Sub-groups (strata) proportional to population.
    *   **Volunteer:** Self-selecting (e.g., adverts), motivation bias.
3.  **Evaluation:** Strengths and weaknesses of each.

## Lesson 3: Experimental Design
**Topics:** Experiments & Structured Design
1.  **Types of Experiment:** Laboratory, Field, Natural (definitions & strengths/weaknesses).
2.  **Experimental Designs:**
    *   **Independent Groups:** No order effects but participant variables.
    *   **Repeated Measures:** Eliminates participant variables but order effects (fatigue/practice).
    *   **Matched Pairs:** Reduces variables/no order effects, but time-consuming.
3.  **Procedures:** Standardised procedures, Random allocation, Counterbalancing (to fix order effects).

## Lesson 4: Non-Experimental Methods
**Topics:** Self-Repoort & Case Studies
1.  **Interviews:** Structured (pre-set questions) vs Unstructured (free discussion).
2.  **Questionnaires:** Open (qualitative) vs Closed (quantitative) questions. Social desirability bias.
3.  **Case Studies:** Detailed study of individual/group, longitudinal nature, difficulty generalizing.

## Lesson 5: Observation & Correlation
**Topics:** Watching & Measuring Relationships
1.  **Observations:** Natural vs Controlled behavior.
2.  **Reliability:** Inter-observer reliability (comparing records).
3.  **Correlations:**
    *   Definition: Association between two variables.
    *   Types: Positive, Negative, Zero.
    *   Scattergrams: Visualizing the data.
    *   Strength/Weakness: Can't establish cause & effect.

## Lesson 6: Ethical Considerations
**Topics:** BPS Guidelines
1.  **The Conflict:** Research needs vs Participant rights.
2.  **Key Issues:** Informed Consent, Deception, Protection from Harm, Right to Withdraw, Confidentiality/Anonymity.
3.  **Dealing with Issues:** Debriefing, Presumptive consent, Ethics committees.

## Lesson 7: Data Handling I
**Topics:** Types of Data & Averages
1.  **Data Types:** Quantitative vs Qualitative, Primary vs Secondary.
2.  **Measures of Central Tendency:**
    *   **Mean:** Add & divide.
    *   **Median:** Middle value.
    *   **Mode:** Most frequent.
3.  **Measure of Dispersion:** Range (Highest - Lowest).

## Lesson 8: Data Handling II
**Topics:** Maths & Graphs
1.  **Maths Skills:** Ratios, Fractions, Percentages.
2.  **Graphs:** Bar Charts (categories), Histograms (continuous), Scattergrams.
3.  **Distributions:** Normal Distribution (bell curve characteristics).

# ðŸ“• Part 2: A-Level Research Methods Course
**Target Audience:** A-Level Students  
**Total Duration:** 11 Lessons (1 Hour Each)

## Lesson 1: The Scientific Approach
**Topics:** Science & Hypotheses
1.  **Psychology as Science:** Nomothetic (universal laws) vs Ideographic (unique) views.
2.  **Scientific Features:** Replicability, Objectivity, Falsification (Popper).
3.  **Hypotheses:** Directional (One-tailed) vs Non-Directional (Two-tailed), Null vs Experimental.

## Lesson 2: Control & Validity
**Topics:** Managing the Study
1.  **Variables:** IV/DV, Extraneous vs Confounding variables.
2.  **Threats to Validity:** Demand Characteristics (Social desirability, "Screw you" effect), Investigator Effects.
3.  **Techniques:** Single-blind vs Double-blind procedures, Randomisation, Standardisation.

## Lesson 3: Advanced Experimental Design
**Topics:** Methods & Design
1.  **Experimental Methods:** Lab, Field, Natural (Quasi).
2.  **Designs:** Independent, Repeated, Matched Pairs.
3.  **Sampling:** Random, Opportunity, Volunteer, Stratified (Detailed evaluation).
4.  **Pilot Studies:** Purpose and importance.

## Lesson 4: Reliability & Validity (Deep Dive)
**Topics:** Quality Assessment
1.  **Reliability:** Inter-rater, Internal (consistency), External (Test-retest).
2.  **Validity Types:** Internal, External (Population, Ecological, Temporal), Face, Concurrent, Construct.
3.  **Improving them:** How to establish and check each type.

## Lesson 5: Ethics & Professional Standards
**Topics:** BPS Code
1.  **BPS Principles:** Respect, Competence, Responsibility, Integrity.
2.  **Specific Issues:** Consent, Deception, Harm, Withdrawal, Confidentiality.
3.  **Management:** Debriefing, Ethics committees, Cost-benefit analysis.

## Lesson 6: Observational Techniques
**Topics:** Systematic Observation
1.  **Types:** Participant vs Non-participant, Overt vs Covert.
2.  **Sampling Behaviour:** Event Sampling vs Time Sampling.
3.  **Reliability:** Establishing Inter-rater reliability (Correlating scores).

## Lesson 7: Self-Report & Beyond
**Topics:** Gathering Data
1.  **Interviews:** Structured, Semi-structured, Unstructured.
2.  **Questionnaires:** Design principles (Open/Closed, Likert scales, avoiding ambiguity).
3.  **Other Methods:** Content Analysis (Thematic), Case Studies, Longitudinal vs Cross-sectional.

## Lesson 8: Descriptive Statistics
**Topics:** Analysing the Raw Data
1.  **Central Tendency:** Mean, Median, Mode (Strengths/Weaknesses).
2.  **Dispersion:** Range, Semi-interquartile range, Standard Deviation.
3.  **Distributions:** Normal, Positively functional Skewed, Negatively Skewed.

## Lesson 9: Inferential Statistics I
**Topics:** The Logic of Testing
1.  **Levels of Data (NOIR):** Nominal, Ordinal, Interval, Ratio.
2.  **Test Types:** Parametric vs Non-parametric (Power & criteria).
3.  **Significance:** p<0.05 vs p<0.01.
4.  **Errors:** Type 1 (Optimistic/False Positive) vs Type 2 (Pessimistic/False Negative).

## Lesson 10: Inferential Statistics II
**Topics:** The Tests
1.  **Choosing a Test:** Flowchart logic (Difference/Correlation -> Design -> Data Level).
2.  **The Tests:** Chi-Square, Sign Test, Mann-Whitney, Wilcoxon, Spearman, Pearson, T-Tests.
3.  **The Sign Test:** Step-by-step calculation practice.

## Lesson 11: Professional Context
**Topics:** Reporting & Economy
1.  **Peer Review:** Process and purpose (Validation, Allocation of funding).
2.  **Report Writing:** Abstract, Intro, Method, Results, Discussion, Refs (Harvard).
3.  **Economy:** Implications of research (Mental health, workforce efficiency).
