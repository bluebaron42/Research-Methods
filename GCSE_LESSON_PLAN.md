- What is it?

### Slide 3: Elaboration (Mechanics)
- How does it work?

- 5 scenario-based questions
- Randomized answer options
- Tests slides 2-3 content

- Famous studies using this method
- Real-world examples

- Focus: Reliability, Validity, Ethics, Economy

- Exam-style scenario question
- Structure guidance (State ‚Üí Explain ‚Üí Context)

## üìù Lesson 1: Hypotheses and Variables

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define and distinguish between IV and DV
- Write operationalised hypotheses
- Understand null vs alternative hypotheses

**Minigame: Hypothesis Lab**
- Students are presented with research scenarios
- Task: Identify the IV, DV, and write both hypotheses
- Drag-and-drop interface with instant feedback

**Key Example:** Balls in bucket experiment (audience vs alone)

---

## üìù Lesson 2: Extraneous Variables

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify extraneous variables in studies
- Explain how EVs affect validity
- Understand standardised procedures and randomisation

**Minigame: EV Hunter**
- Visual lab environment
- Students click on potential EVs (noise, temperature, lighting, etc.)
- Must suggest control methods

**Key Example:** Coffee and performance study

---

## üìù Lesson 3: Types of Experiment

**Learning Objectives:**
- Compare lab, field, and natural experiments
- Evaluate strengths and weaknesses
- Match methods to research aims

**Minigame: Experiment Designer**
- Given 5 research aims
- Students select appropriate experiment type
- Justify their choice with strengths/weaknesses

**Key Studies:**
- Lab: Loftus & Palmer (Memory)
- Field: Hofling (Nurses)
- Natural: Hurricane effects on stress

---

## üìù Lesson 4: Experimental Designs

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between independent groups, repeated measures, matched pairs
- Understand counterbalancing
- Identify when to use each design

**Minigame: Design Matcher**
- Scenario cards appear
- Students choose: IG, RM, or MP
- Explain how they would deal with order effects or participant variables

---

## üìù Lesson 5: Sampling Methods

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain random, opportunity, systematic, and stratified sampling
- Calculate stratified samples
- Evaluate representativeness

**Minigame: Participant Selector**
- Target population displayed (e.g., school of 500)
- Build a sample of 50 using different methods
- See how representative each method is

**Key Example:** School population (teachers 10%, students 80%, staff 10%)

---

## üìù Lesson 6: Ethical Considerations

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify BPS ethical guidelines
- Recognise ethical violations
- Suggest solutions (consent forms, debriefing)

**Minigame: Ethics Checker**
- Research scenarios with ethical problems
- Students identify issues (consent, deception, harm, privacy, confidentiality)
- Propose solutions

**Key Example:** Unsolvable puzzle study (deception & harm)

---

## üìù Lesson 7: Interviews and Questionnaires

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish open vs closed questions
- Understand Likert scales and rating scales
- Compare structured, semi-structured, unstructured interviews

**Minigame: Question Writer**
- Design a 5-question survey
- Must include open and closed questions
- Feedback on question quality (bias, leading questions)

---

## üìù Lesson 8: Observation Studies

**Learning Objectives:**
- Create behavioural categories
- Understand covert/overt and participant/non-participant
- Calculate inter-observer reliability

**Minigame: Behaviour Coder**
- Watch video of children playing
- Code behaviours into categories (aggression, cooperation, etc.)
- Compare with expert coding

---

## üìù Lesson 9: Correlations

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify positive, negative, and zero correlations
- Plot scatterplots
- Understand "correlation ‚â† causation"

**Minigame: Scatterplot Builder**
- Given data pairs (ice cream sales vs drownings)
- Plot the points
- Interpret the relationship
- Identify intervening variables

---

## üìù Lesson 10: Case Studies

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain the case study method
- Understand longitudinal vs cross-sectional
- Evaluate generalisability

**Minigame: Case Study Detective**
- Explore HM (amnesia patient)
- Extract qualitative and quantitative data
- Answer questions about his condition

**Key Example:** HM (Henry Molaison)

---

## üìù Lesson 11: Reliability and Validity

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define reliability and validity
- Apply test-retest method
- Distinguish internal vs ecological validity

**Minigame: Method Evaluator**
- Rate studies on reliability and validity scales
- Justify ratings
- Suggest improvements

---

## üìù Lesson 12: Types of Data

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish quantitative vs qualitative
- Compare primary vs secondary data
- Evaluate strengths and weaknesses

**Minigame: Data Classifier**
- Drag data examples into correct categories
- Speed challenge

---

## üìù Lesson 13: Descriptive Statistics

**Learning Objectives:**
- Calculate mean, median, mode
- Calculate range
- Choose appropriate measure

**Minigame: Stats Calculator**
- Data sets appear
- Students calculate all measures of central tendency
- Timed challenge

---

## üìù Lesson 14: Interpretation and Display of Data

**Learning Objectives:**
- Create histograms and bar charts
- Understand normal distribution
- Choose appropriate graph type

**Minigame: Graph Builder**
- Given data, select correct chart type
- Build the graph
- Interpret patterns

---

## üìù Lesson 15: Computation and Arithmetic

**Learning Objectives:**
- Convert decimals to fractions
- Calculate percentages and ratios
- Use significant figures and standard form

**Minigame: Maths Challenge**
- Rapid-fire calculation questions
- Levels increase in difficulty
- Leaderboard
